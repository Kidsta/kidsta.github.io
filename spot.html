Spot

Designing a Memecoin Trading App

2024 - 2025

The Challenge

Joining Spot mid-flight, I was tasked with refining an early-stage, fast-paced startup design into a more mature product. The initial goal was to make memecoin trading as intuitive as buying Bitcoin on Coinbase, focusing on a simple happy path. My challenge was to handle the complex edges and ensure the product felt familiar and easy.

Inherited iOS designs

The Approach

I kept things lean and user-focused, using a tight feedback loop with a small group of testers. Rather than reinventing the wheel, we embraced familiar design patterns that users already knew from other finance apps. I also tailored our design system, adding Figma variables to ensure consistency, speed and cohesion across web and iOS.

The Process

Different tools for different levels of certainty move fast where known, validate where unknown.

Data & Signal

We kept an eye on both data and signals to stay honest about whether our work was actually improving the experience. For new or uncertain features, prototypes gave us early validation and quick ways to improve before shipping. Once live, we leaned on data logs to understand where users were getting stuck.

  • Failure rates: Monitored where transactions failed or timed out.
  • Friction points: Tracked steps that slowed users down in the onramp and trading flows.
  • User feedback: Regular check-ins with a small panel of testers.

One key signal we tracked was trade failure due to price movement. Meme-coins move fast, and often by the time a user confirmed, the quoted price had already changed. To address this, we introduced a slippage control that let users set their own tolerance levels. This gave trades a greater chance of completing successfully while giving users control over how much risk they were willing to accept.

Real-World Working

Day-to-day was a juggling act: close with Head of Product and engineers, switching between iOS and desktop, and keeping designs just ahead of development.

  • Design‚Äìdev pairing: Clarified tricky states (timeouts, slippage, failed swaps) before rework.
  • Multiple tracks: New features, improvements, and system work in the same sprint.
  • Clear handoffs: Simple, precise specs; always available to unblock quickly.
  • Platform parity: Shared mental model with platform-native patterns for each UI.

Outcome

Launched on desktop and iOS; attracted hundreds of users; iterated quickly to stay competitive and trustworthy.

  • Adoption: Smooth onboarding; strong qualitative feedback on clarity.
  • Less friction: Lower failure rates; faster task completion in core flows.
  • Cross-platform consistency: Users could switch devices without relearning.
  • System fit-for-purpose: Leaner components and tokens sped up delivery.
